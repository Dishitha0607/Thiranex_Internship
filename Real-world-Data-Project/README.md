# Retail Sales Data Analysis

## Project Overview

This project focuses on cleaning, analyzing, and visualizing a retail sales dataset to uncover customer purchasing behavior, sales trends, and revenue patterns. The objective is to transform raw transactional data into meaningful business insights using Python and data analysis techniques.

---

## Dataset Information

The dataset contains 1,000 retail transactions with the following attributes:

* Transaction ID
* Date
* Customer ID
* Gender
* Age
* Product Category
* Quantity
* Price per Unit
* Total Amount

### Dataset Size

* Rows: 1000
* Columns: 9

---

## Tools and Libraries Used

* Python
* Pandas
* NumPy
* Matplotlib
* Seaborn

---

## Data Cleaning Process

The following data cleaning steps were performed:

### 1. Dataset Inspection

* Checked dataset structure using `head()`, `shape()`, and `info()`
* Verified column names and data types

### 2. Missing Value Analysis

* Checked for null values using `isnull().sum()`
* No missing values were found in the dataset

### 3. Duplicate Value Analysis

* Checked duplicate records using `duplicated().sum()`
* No duplicate records were found

### 4. Date Formatting

* Converted the Date column into datetime format
* Extracted Month information for time-series analysis

---

## Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

### Product Category Distribution

Analysis of transaction counts across product categories revealed:

* Clothing: 351 transactions
* Electronics: 342 transactions
* Beauty: 307 transactions

**Insight:** Clothing was the most frequently purchased category, indicating strong customer demand.

---

### Revenue by Product Category

Total revenue generated by each category:

* Electronics: 156,905
* Clothing: 155,580
* Beauty: 143,515

**Insight:** Electronics generated the highest revenue despite having fewer transactions than Clothing, suggesting a higher average selling price.

---

### Gender Distribution

Customer transaction distribution:

* Female: 510 transactions
* Male: 490 transactions

**Insight:** The customer base is evenly distributed across genders, indicating balanced customer engagement.

---

### Revenue by Gender

Total revenue generated:

* Female: 232,840
* Male: 223,160

**Insight:** Female customers contributed slightly more revenue than male customers.

---

### Age Distribution Analysis

Customer age statistics:

* Average Age: 41.39 years
* Median Age: 42 years
* Minimum Age: 18 years
* Maximum Age: 64 years

**Insight:** The business serves a diverse customer base spanning multiple age groups with no significant age outliers.

---

### Monthly Sales Trend

Monthly sales analysis revealed notable fluctuations throughout the year.

Highest Revenue Months:

1. May – 53,150
2. October – 46,580
3. December – 44,690

Lowest Revenue Months:

1. September – 23,620
2. March – 28,990

**Insight:** Sales performance varies throughout the year, indicating seasonal purchasing behavior.

---

### Correlation Analysis

Correlation analysis was performed on numerical variables.

Key findings:

* Price per Unit and Total Amount: 0.85 (Strong Positive Correlation)
* Quantity and Total Amount: 0.37 (Moderate Positive Correlation)
* Age and Total Amount: -0.06 (Negligible Correlation)

**Insight:** Product pricing has the strongest influence on total sales revenue, while customer age has little impact on spending behavior.

---

## Key Business Insights

* Clothing recorded the highest number of transactions.
* Electronics generated the highest overall revenue.
* Female customers contributed slightly more revenue than male customers.
* Customers ranged from 18 to 64 years of age, indicating broad market reach.
* May was the highest-performing sales month.
* September recorded the lowest sales performance.
* Product pricing strongly influences total revenue generation.

---

## Conclusion

The retail sales dataset was successfully cleaned, analyzed, and visualized to identify important business trends and customer purchasing patterns. The analysis revealed that Electronics is the highest revenue-generating category, while Clothing is the most frequently purchased category. Customer demographics are balanced across genders and age groups, demonstrating broad market appeal. Monthly sales trends highlighted seasonal variations in revenue, while correlation analysis showed that product pricing is the primary driver of sales value.

These insights can support data-driven decision-making in areas such as inventory management, pricing strategy, marketing campaigns, and customer targeting.
